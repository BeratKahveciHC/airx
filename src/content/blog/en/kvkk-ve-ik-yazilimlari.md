## Why Is Biometric Data So Critical?

Under the Turkish Personal Data Protection Law (KVKK), biometric data is classified as **sensitive personal data**. This means that processing data such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or retina scans is subject to much stricter rules.

Many employers use fingerprint scanners for employee attendance without considering this dimension of KVKK. Yet obtaining explicit consent, disclosing the purpose of data processing, and complying with the proportionality principle are all legal requirements.

## What Are the Legal Risks?

- **Administrative fines:** KVKK violations can result in fines ranging from 50,000 TRY to 1,000,000 TRY.
- **Decisions by the Personal Data Protection Authority:** The Authority has increased its audits of workplaces that process biometric data.
- **Employee complaints:** Biometric data collected without consent gives employees grounds to file a complaint with the Authority.

## The Alternative: Attendance via Mobile QR Code

AiRX makes employee tracking possible without collecting biometric data. Employees clock in and out by scanning a QR code on their smartphone.

**Advantages:**

- No biometric data ever enters the system
- KVKK compliance is built in from the start
- No hardware purchases required
- Compatible with iOS and Android

## Conclusion

Collecting biometric data in the workplace is no longer "standard practice" — it is a serious legal risk. Mobile QR-based attendance systems offer an alternative that is both safer and more respectful of employee privacy.
